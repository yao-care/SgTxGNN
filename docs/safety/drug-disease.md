# Drug-Disease Precautions

Conditions where certain medications require caution or are contraindicated.

---

## Overview

Drug-disease interactions (DDSIs) occur when a medication worsens an existing condition or when a disease state affects drug action. These are critical considerations for drug repurposing.

---

## Categories

### Absolute Contraindications

Medications that should NOT be used in certain conditions:

| Drug Class | Contraindicated Condition | Reason |
|------------|-------------------------|--------|
| NSAIDs | Active GI bleeding | Worsens bleeding |
| Beta-blockers | Severe asthma | Bronchospasm risk |
| Metformin | Severe renal impairment | Lactic acidosis |
| ACE inhibitors | Angioedema history | Recurrence risk |

### Relative Contraindications

Medications requiring careful risk-benefit assessment:

| Drug Class | Precaution Condition | Consideration |
|------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Anticoagulants | Fall risk | Bleeding risk |
| Antipsychotics | Dementia | Mortality increase |
| Corticosteroids | Diabetes | Glucose elevation |
| Thiazides | Gout | Uric acid increase |

## Organ-Specific Precautions

### Renal Impairment

| Drug | Concern | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| Metformin | Lactic acidosis | Reduce dose or avoid |
| NSAIDs | Worsening function | Avoid |
| Aminoglycosides | Nephrotoxicity | Dose adjust |

### Hepatic Impairment

| Drug | Concern | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| Paracetamol | Hepatotoxicity | Limit dose |
| Statins | Elevated enzymes | Monitor LFTs |
| Methotrexate | Hepatic fibrosis | Avoid if severe |

### Cardiac Conditions

| Condition | Drugs to Avoid | Reason |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Heart failure | NSAIDs, Thiazolidinediones | Fluid retention |
| Long QT | QT-prolonging drugs | Arrhythmia |
| Bradycardia | Beta-blockers, Digoxin | Worsening |

## Special Populations

### Elderly

| Consideration | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| Anticholinergic burden | Antihistamines, TCAs |
| Fall risk medications | Benzodiazepines, Antihypertensives |
| Renal dose adjustment | Most drugs cleared renally |

### Pregnancy

| Category | Drugs to Avoid |
|----------|---------------|
| Teratogenic | Methotrexate, Warfarin, Retinoids |
| Third trimester | NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors |

### Paediatric

| Consideration | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| Contraindicated | Tetracyclines, Fluoroquinolones |
| Dose adjustment | Most medications |

## Using This Information

### For Repurposing Research

When considering a drug for a new indication:

1. Review all DDSIs for the drug
2. Consider the target population
3. Assess if repurposed use changes risk profile
4. Plan appropriate monitoring

### Clinical Relevance

DDSIs may be:
- **Direct**: Drug worsens condition
- **Indirect**: Condition affects drug metabolism
- **Monitoring-based**: Requires closer follow-up
