# Drug-Food Interactions

How food and beverages can affect medication efficacy and safety.

---

## Overview

Drug-food interactions can:
- Alter drug absorption
- Change drug metabolism
- Affect drug distribution
- Impact clinical outcomes

---

## Common Interactions

### Grapefruit

One of the most clinically significant food-drug interactions.

| Drug Class | Examples | Effect |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Statins | Simvastatin, Atorvastatin | Increased levels, myopathy |
| Calcium channel blockers | Felodipine, Nifedipine | Hypotension |
| Immunosuppressants | Cyclosporine, Tacrolimus | Toxicity |
| Antihistamines | Terfenadine | QT prolongation |

**Mechanism**: Inhibits intestinal CYP3A4 and P-glycoprotein

### Dairy Products

| Drug | Effect | Recommendation |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Tetracyclines | Reduced absorption | Separate by 2 hours |
| Fluoroquinolones | Reduced absorption | Separate by 2 hours |
| Bisphosphonates | Reduced absorption | Take on empty stomach |

**Mechanism**: Calcium chelation

### High-Fat Meals

| Drug | Effect | Timing |
|------|--------|--------|
| Isotretinoin | Enhanced absorption | Take with food |
| Griseofulvin | Enhanced absorption | Take with fatty food |
| Some HIV drugs | Altered absorption | Check specific guidance |

### Vitamin K Foods

| Foods | Drugs Affected | Clinical Impact |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| Green leafy vegetables | Warfarin | Reduced anticoagulation |
| Liver | Warfarin | INR variability |
| Green tea | Warfarin | Monitor INR |

## Beverages

### Alcohol

| Drug Class | Interaction |
|------------|-------------|
| Metronidazole | Disulfiram reaction |
| Metformin | Lactic acidosis risk |
| Paracetamol | Hepatotoxicity |
| CNS depressants | Enhanced sedation |
| Antihypertensives | Hypotension |

### Caffeine

| Drug | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Theophylline | Both metabolised by CYP1A2 |
| Fluoroquinolones | Inhibit caffeine metabolism |
| Lithium | Caffeine withdrawal increases levels |

### Milk

See Dairy Products above.

## Timing Recommendations

### Take on Empty Stomach

(1 hour before or 2 hours after food)

| Drug | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Bisphosphonates | Avoid calcium chelation |
| Levothyroxine | Maximise absorption |
| Some antibiotics | Food reduces absorption |

### Take with Food

| Drug | Reason |
|------|--------|
| NSAIDs | Reduce GI irritation |
| Metformin | Reduce GI side effects |
| Griseofulvin | Enhance absorption |

---

## Special Diets

### High-Protein Diet

- May affect L-dopa absorption
- Can alter drug binding
- May induce certain CYP enzymes

### High-Fibre Diet

- May reduce drug absorption
- Can bind to certain medications
- Timing separation recommended

### Enteral Nutrition

| Consideration | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| Drug-formula compatibility | Check interactions |
| Timing of administration | Separate if needed |
| Tube occlusion | Use appropriate formulations |

---

## Patient Counselling Points

1. **Consistency**: Maintain consistent dietary habits with drugs like warfarin
2. **Timing**: Follow specific timing instructions
3. **Grapefruit**: Avoid with susceptible medications
4. **Alcohol**: Check for interactions
5. **Supplements**: Report all supplements to healthcare providers
